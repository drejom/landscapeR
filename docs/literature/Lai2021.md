# Lai et al. 2021 — RAMP2-AS1 Regulates Endothelial Homeostasis and Aging

**Journal**: Frontiers in Cell and Developmental Biology 2021;9:635307  
**DOI**: 10.3389/fcell.2021.635307  
**Authors**: Chih-Hung Lai, Aleysha T. Chen, Andrew B. Burns, Kiran Sriram, Yingjun Luo, Xiaofang Tang, Sergio Branciamore, Denis O'Meally, Szu-Ling Chang, Po-Hsun Huang, John Y-J. Shyy, Shu Chien, Russell C. Rockne, Zhen Bouman Chen  
**Data**: GEO accessions GSE103672, GSE136912, GSE163433

---

## One-sentence summary

Applied the SVD/PCA eigengene framework from Rockne2020 to time-series RNA-seq from endothelial cells (ECs) under physiological pulsatile shear stress (PS) vs. pathophysiological oscillatory shear stress (OS), identifying RAMP2 and its antisense lncRNA RAMP2-AS1 as top candidates driving divergent EC aging trajectories — the first application of the method outside haematological cancer.

---

## What makes this paper different from the leukemia trilogy

This paper applies **only the state-space / eigengene portion** of the framework — it stops before the quasi-potential and Fokker-Planck steps. There is no double-well potential, no critical point estimation, and no time-to-event prediction. The contribution is:

1. Demonstrating SVD/PCA aging trajectories in a **non-cancer, non-hematopoietic** context (vascular endothelium)
2. Running **separate analyses for protein-coding genes (PCGs) and non-coding genes (NCGs)**
3. Adding **mutual information** as a complementary gene-ranking method alongside eigengene magnitude
4. Providing functional validation (LNA knockdown, sprouting assay, SA-β-gal senescence staining) linking a PCA-identified candidate to a phenotype

---

## Biological question

Do the temporal transcriptome dynamics of ECs under PS and OS follow divergent trajectories quantifiable by PCA, and can eigengene ranking identify novel regulators of EC aging that are not known from candidate-gene approaches?

## Dataset

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Cell type | Human umbilical vein endothelial cells (HUVECs), passages 4–8, pooled donors |
| Conditions | PS: 12 ± 2 dyne/cm² pulsatile shear (atheroprotective); OS: 1 ± 5 dyne/cm² oscillatory shear (atheroprone) |
| Time points | 10 points: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 16, 24 hours |
| Replicates | 2 biological replicates per condition (OS1, OS2, PS1, PS2) |
| Validation stimuli | Atorvastatin (ATV, 1–5 µM, 24h) and TNFα (100 ng/ml, 24h) used to cross-reference trajectory positions |
| Additional data | Hypoxia-treated human microvascular ECs (GSE136912); human mesenteric arterial intima (n=44 donors) |
| RNA-seq processing | STAR → GRCh38/GENCODE release 33; Subread featureCounts; DESeq2 for DE analysis |

## Algorithm: step-by-step

### 1. Data matrices — separate PCG and NCG analyses

Two data matrices constructed:

- **PCG matrix**: rows = samples, columns = protein-coding genes; values = log2 CPM, mean-centred
- **NCG matrix**: rows = samples, columns = non-coding genes (primarily lncRNAs); values = log2 CPM, mean-centred

### 2. SVD → state-space

```
X = U Σ V*   (same formulation as Rockne2020)
```

PCA run on each matrix independently. First 8 PCs examined.

**PCG results:**
- PC1: correlated with time (both PS and OS) — captures shared temporal dynamics
- PC2: intermittent spiking associated with mitochondrial gene expression
- **PC3**: showed **divergent trajectories** between PS and OS — selected as the "aging trajectory" axis

**NCG results:**
- PC1: correlated with time
- **PC2**: showed divergent PS vs. OS trajectories — selected as the aging trajectory axis for NCGs

The choice of PC3 (PCGs) and PC2 (NCGs) departs from the leukemia papers' PC2 selection — here the criterion is simply: which PC gives the greatest separation between conditions?

### 3. Eigengene ranking

For each gene, the eigengene = loading on the aging-trajectory PC (PC3 for PCGs, PC2 for NCGs):

```
eigengene_j = V*[j, aging_PC]
```

Genes ranked in **descending order by absolute value** of their eigengene. Top 200 PCGs and top NCGs listed.

Projection of additional datasets into the PC space:

```
X' → projection = X' · V*
```

Used to place ATV-treated and TNFα-treated samples into the PS/OS state-space without refitting.

- ATV-treated samples mapped close to PS trajectories (consistent with atheroprotective effect)
- TNFα-treated samples mapped close to OS trajectories (consistent with pro-inflammatory / aging effect)
- Hypoxia-treated samples appeared intermediate (expected: unclear effect on EC aging)

### 4. Mutual information ranking (complementary)

In addition to eigengene magnitude, genes were ranked by **mutual information** with respect to flow condition:

```
MI(gene_j; flow_condition)
```

Using the Gao et al. 2017 method for combining discrete (condition label) and continuous (expression) variables. Top 50 PCGs and NCGs by MI reported in Supplementary Table 3.

Key finding: genes highly ranked by eigengene (e.g., KLF2, KLF4) were also highly ranked by MI (KLF: rank #1 and #12 by MI), supporting cross-validation of the PCA-based ranking.

### 5. Gene selection criteria for lncRNA candidates

lncRNA candidates prioritised by cross-referencing top NCG eigengenes with a sign criterion:

- **PS-associated**: positive FC with PS/OS AND positive FC with ATV/control
- **OS-associated**: negative FC with PS/OS AND negative FC with ATV/control (i.e., suppressed by the atheroprotective statin, consistent with OS/aging)

The top three NCG candidates meeting this criterion: MIR503HG, C1orf132, **RAMP2-AS1** (ranked #11 in NCG list).

RAMP2 was separately identified at rank #19 in the PCG list.

### 6. No quasi-potential step

Unlike Rockne2020–Frankhouser2024, this paper does **not** estimate a quasi-potential, identify critical points, or apply the Fokker-Planck equation. The analysis stops at eigengene-ranked state-space trajectories. Time-to-event prediction is not performed.

## Biological findings (brief — not the focus for landscapeR)

- **RAMP2-AS1**: lncRNA on antisense strand of RAMP2 (chr 17:40,894,172–40,919,137); transcribed divergently from RAMP2
- RAMP2 and RAMP2-AS1 expression strongly correlated in HUVECs (R = 0.87, P < 0.001) and in human mesenteric arteries (r = 0.55, P < 0.001)
- LNA knockdown of RAMP2-AS1 → decreased RAMP2 mRNA and protein → increased SA-β-gal (senescence marker), impaired 3D sprouting (angiogenesis), modest trend toward apoptosis
- RNA-seq after RAMP2-AS1 knockdown: 252 DEGs (P < 0.05), 585 DEGs (P < 0.1); top enriched pathways: angiogenesis, inflammatory response, MAP kinase activity, unfolded protein response (UPR), ECM organisation
- Key downregulated pro-angiogenic genes: VEGFA, PDGFA, FGF, ADM2
- Key upregulated pro-inflammatory genes: CXCL11, CCL14, MIF

## Relationship to landscapeR

| Lai2021 feature | landscapeR relevance |
|---|---|
| SVD applied to non-cancer, non-hematopoietic data (EC aging) | Confirms the SVD → state-space framework is biology-agnostic; relevant for Pogona TSD and diabetes instantiations |
| Separate PCG and NCG analyses | Precedent for treating different gene-class layers as separate matrices (relevant to Stage 1 multi-omic decomposition strategy) |
| PC selection by condition separation, not variance | The axis-selection criterion is a design choice in Stage 1 that must be configurable — variance-maximising and condition-separating PCs are not always the same |
| Mutual information as a complementary ranking | Possible addition to Stage 2 output: eigengene ranking could be supplemented with MI-based ranking for robustness |
| Projection of external datasets using training V* | Confirms the projection approach (X' · V*_training) generalises across datasets; standard operation in landscapeR Stage 1 output |
| No quasi-potential step | Demonstrates the pipeline is usable in partial form — Stage 1 output (state-space + eigengenes) is independently valuable even without Stage 2 |
| 10 time points × 2 replicates | Smallest dataset in the corpus — useful as a stress test for Stage 0 thinness sweep (what is the minimum sample size for reliable quasi-potential estimation?) |

## Method variants relative to Rockne2020

| Aspect | Rockne2020 | Lai2021 |
|---|---|---|
| Data type | Bulk RNA-seq, mouse PBMC | Bulk RNA-seq, human HUVECs in vitro |
| Comparison axis | Disease vs. healthy (PC2) | PS vs. OS shear condition (PC3 for PCGs, PC2 for NCGs) |
| Replicates | n=7 mice per group | n=2 per condition |
| Time points | Up to 10 months | 0–24 hours |
| Gene classes | All genes together | PCGs and NCGs separately |
| Gene ranking | Eigengene magnitude (v₂*) | Eigengene magnitude + mutual information |
| Quasi-potential | Yes — double-well polynomial | Not performed |
| Critical points | Yes — c₁*, c₁, c₂, c₃ | Not identified |
| Fokker-Planck | Yes — time-to-disease prediction | Not performed |
| External validation | Two independent mouse cohorts | ATV/TNFα treated cells; human arterial intima |
